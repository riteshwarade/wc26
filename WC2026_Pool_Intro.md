## About the World Cup

The FIFA Men's World Cup is arguably the world's greatest sporting event. The 2026 version drew 206 teams into a two-year qualifying campaign and will culminate in 48 national teams playing 104 matches in 16 cities over 38 days, with the first game on June 11 and the final on July 19. For the first time ever, the tournament is hosted by three countries: the USA, Canada, and Mexico. The expanded field with 48 teams (up from 32) means more underdogs and more upsets.

Only 8 teams have ever won the World Cup: Brazil (5), Italy (4), Germany (4), Argentina (3), Uruguay (2), France (2), England (1), and Spain (1). All but Italy will feature in this tournament. Four teams make their World Cup debut: Cabo Verde (Cape Verde), Curaçao, Jordan, and Uzbekistan.

## The Tournament

The World Cup is organized in two stages: Group and Knockout.

- **Group stage:** 12 groups of 4 teams each. Each team plays 3 matches. Each 90-minute match can end in a win for either team or a draw. The top two from each group, plus 8 of the 12 best 3rd-placed teams, qualify for the next stage.
- **Knockout stage:** The 32 teams are set in a march-madness style bracket. Each match must end with a winner (no draws), so if teams are tied after 90 minutes, they play an extra 30 minutes, with a penalty shootout if still tied.

## Our Pool

Our pool follows the same two-stage structure. 

- **Group stage:** Pick the correct result (win for either team or draw) for all 72 group stage matches. Two points for each correct pick.
- **Knockout stage:** Pick the winner of each knockout game across 5 rounds, all the way to the final. Progressively more points for each round, starting with 4 points for the Round of 32, up to 24 points for the Final. One catch: if a team you picked for a later round gets knocked out earlier, that pick scores 0. So choose wisely.
